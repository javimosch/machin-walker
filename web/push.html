machin-walker — push recovery

The same tinybrain walker (13 sensors → 16 → 4), warm-started from the certified walk and fine-tuned to survive sagittal shoves. Recovery is blind — no new sensors: a shove is a velocity impulse to the torso, and the net feels it only through its existing balance senses (torso pitch, pitch-rate, hip velocity), then catches itself. It's shoved at 1.7 m/s every couple of seconds, fore and aft — the untrained walker collapses by ~1.3 m/s. Physics in WebAssembly; JS only draws. Shove it yourself with the buttons or arrow keys.
